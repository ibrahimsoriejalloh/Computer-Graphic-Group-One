What you are learning

This tool demonstrates the core pipeline of modern computer graphics using WebGL. You can interactively explore:

  • 3D geometry transformations: translation, rotation, and scaling of a 3D object.
  • View transformation: camera position and its effect on the scene.
  • Projection: difference between perspective and orthographic projections.
  • Lighting: simple ambient and diffuse shading on a cube.

How this supports geometry / physics learning

  • Mathematical foundations: uses 4×4 matrices for 3D transformations and projections.
  • Spatial reasoning: helps students see how moving and rotating shapes changes their position and orientation in space.
  • Projection and viewing: shows how the same 3D object can look different under perspective and orthographic views.
  • Illumination & shading: illustrates how light direction affects the appearance of surfaces.
  • Interactive exploration: learners experiment with controls to build intuition about 3D geometry and simple physics-style scenes.
